Trust

Walking in Faith

— Pastor Sarah Williams

Key Verse

"Trust in the Lord with all your heart and lean not on your own understanding."

Proverbs 3:5

Faith is not the absence of doubt, but the choice to trust God despite our circumstances. In our daily lives, we face countless situations that challenge our confidence in divine providence. Whether it's financial uncertainties, health concerns, relationship struggles, or career decisions, our natural instinct is to rely on our own understanding and control.

However, the proverb we're reflecting on today calls us to something radically different. "Trust in the Lord with all your heart" doesn't mean superficial belief or occasional prayer. It means a deep, foundational surrender of our will to God's wisdom. It means acknowledging that the infinite God of the universe sees what we cannot see, knows what we do not know, and loves us infinitely.

"Lean not on your own understanding" is the second part of this powerful truth. Our understanding is limited. We see only the present moment and our immediate circumstances. We cannot see how today's struggle will contribute to tomorrow's strength, or how apparent setbacks are actually divine redirections.

When we trust God completely, we experience a peace that transcends understanding. This peace doesn't come from having all the answers or a clear path forward. It comes from knowing that we don't have to figure everything out on our own. It comes from the assurance that God is faithful, that He works all things together for good for those who love Him.

As you reflect on this devotional today, consider one area in your life where you've been relying too heavily on your own understanding. Is it worry about the future? Anxiety about control? Fear of the unknown? Whatever it is, this is your invitation to release it into God's capable hands and experience the freedom that comes from true faith.

Reflect

Where have you been leaning on your own understanding instead of trusting God? What specific situation or decision do you need to surrender to Him? Write down one thing you're worried about controlling, and commit to releasing it to God's care this week.
